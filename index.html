India Leading Minerals/Ores

Coal

Coal is used as a feedstock for various chemical processes, such as coal gasification and coal-to-chemicals conversion

Iron Ore

India is a major producer of iron ore, which is primarily used in the production of steel. Steel is a vital material for constructing infrastructure, machinery, and transportation equipment, all of which are essential for the chemical and petrochemical sectors.

Copper Ore

copper ore, which is used in the production of copper metal and various copper-based chemicals. Copper is widely used in electrical wiring, plumbing, and industrial machinery, making it essential for the chemical and petrochemical industries.

Bauxite

India has significant reserves of bauxite, the primary ore used for aluminum production. Bauxite is utilized in the production of alumina, which is further processed into aluminum metal for various applications in the chemical and manufacturing industries.

Potash

India imports potash, a potassium-containing mineral, for use in fertilizer production. Potassium is an essential nutrient for plant growth and is used in various chemical formulations to improve agricultural productivity.

Limestone:

India has abundant reserves of limestone, which is used as a raw material for the production of lime, cement, and various chemical products. Lime is used in processes such as water treatment, metallurgy, and sugar refining, while cement is essential for construction projects.

Market size in India ?

The petrochemical and chemical industry in India is estimated to be worth over $150 billion, making it one of the largest chemical markets in the Asia-Pacific region.The industry encompasses a wide range of products, including basic chemicals, polymers, specialty chemicals, fertilizers, pharmaceuticals, and agrochemicals, catering to diverse industrial sectors.India's chemical industry accounts for approximately 3% of the global chemical market and is expected to grow at a CAGR of around 8-10% over the next decade.Petrochemicals, including ethylene, propylene, polyethylene, polypropylene, and PVC, constitute a significant portion of the Indian chemical market, driven by demand from the packaging, construction, and automotive sectors.The Indian specialty chemicals sector is witnessing robust growth, driven by increasing demand for high-performance chemicals, additives, and intermediates from industries such as textiles, paints, coatings, and personal care
